Professional Materials & Standards

Every Tillerstead installation is built to meet or exceed national tile-setting standards. For waterproofing in showers, tubs, and wet areas, we use membranes that comply with ANSI A118.10: Load-Bearing, Bonded Waterproof Membranes , helping ensure moisture stays where it belongs—away from your framing and subfloor.

All layouts, coverage, and movement control follow ANSI A108 installation standards and TCNA EJ171 movement-joint guidelines . These standards define proper substrate prep, full mortar coverage, and flexible perimeter joints—preventing cracks, debonding, and tenting over time.

In short: the work you see is built on what you don’t see—sound structure, bonded waterproofing, and industry-informed technique.

Do you work with large-format tile?

Yes. Large-format tile demands very flat substrates, proper mortar selection, and the right trowel techniques. We follow ANSI A108 and TCNA EJ171 guidance so big tile installs stay flat and secure over time.

Do you waterproof behind and under tile?

Yes. In showers, tub surrounds, and other high-moisture areas, we install membranes that meet the ANSI A118.10 waterproofing standard . That’s what keeps water out of your framing and subfloor for the long term.

What are movement joints, and why do they matter?

Movement joints are flexible gaps built into tile installations to absorb expansion and contraction from temperature, humidity, or building movement. Instead of rigid grout, these joints use flexible sealant—often 100% silicone— to allow slight movement without cracking.

We install perimeter, change-of-plane, and field joints according to TCNA EJ171 recommendations and ANSI A108 installation standards , helping protect your tile system from cracks and tenting.
