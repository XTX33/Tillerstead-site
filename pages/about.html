Professional Materials & Standards

Every Tillerstead installation is built to meet or exceed national tile-setting standards. In wet areas — showers, tubs, and other high-moisture zones — we use membranes that comply with ANSI A118.10 load-bearing, bonded waterproofing requirements , helping keep moisture away from framing, subfloor, and adjacent finishes.

Layout, coverage, and movement control follow ANSI A108 installation standards and TCNA EJ171 movement-joint guidelines . These define proper substrate prep, full mortar coverage, and flexible perimeter joints — the details that prevent cracks, debonding, and tenting over time.

In short: what you see is backed by what you don’t see — sound structure, bonded waterproofing, and industry-informed technique.

Do you work with large-format tile?

Yes. Large-format tile demands very flat substrates, the right mortar, and the right trowel techniques. Tillerstead follows ANSI A108 and TCNA EJ171 guidance so big tile installs stay flat, bonded, and in plane over time.

Do you waterproof behind and under tile?

Yes. In showers, tub surrounds, and other high-moisture areas, we install membranes that meet the ANSI A118.10 waterproofing standard . That’s what keeps water out of your framing and subfloor for the long term.

What are movement joints, and why do they matter?

Movement joints are flexible gaps built into tile installations to absorb expansion and contraction from temperature, humidity, or building movement. Instead of rigid grout, these joints use flexible sealant — often 100% silicone — so the system can move slightly without cracking.

Tillerstead installs perimeter, change-of-plane, and field joints according to TCNA EJ171 and ANSI A108 , helping protect your tile system from cracks and tenting.
