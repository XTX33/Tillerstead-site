Shower Systems

From full tear-outs to new custom builds, showers are rebuilt from framing out — with bonded waterproofing, proper slope, and finishes that actually belong in a wet area.

Bonded Waterproofing & Pans

Traditional or foam pans with ANSI A118.10 bonded membranes, proper pre-slope, curbs, and niches. Flood testing available so you know the system holds before tile goes in.

Large-Format Shower & Bath Layouts

Flat substrates, correct mortar and trowel technique, and movement joints where they belong. Clean transitions at glass, fixtures, and floors for showers and baths that look built-in, not patched.

Floors & Walls

Tile that stays quiet underfoot and true on the wall — built on solid prep, not just “good enough” thinset over whatever is there.

Floor Prep, Substrates & Tile

Self-leveling, crack isolation, and underlayments set the base for porcelain, ceramic, mosaic, and stone. Layouts are aligned, cuts are clean, and grout lines stay straight across rooms.

Premium Stone & Large-Format Porcelain

Natural stone and large-format porcelain installed to manufacturer specs — with matched setting and grout systems — for baths and living areas that need both durability and a finished look.

Backsplashes

Kitchens and wet walls that look intentional: clean outlets, level lines, and edges finished so the tile belongs to the room, not just the wall behind it.

Kitchen Backsplashes & Features

Outlets cut tight, edges sealed, and patterns aligned — stacked, staggered, herringbone, or hex. Great for light refreshes or as part of a larger kitchen update.

Repairs & Problem Solving

Not every project needs a full gut. Some just need honest diagnostics and targeted work so you know whether to repair, rebuild, or plan for a later remodel.

Leak & Grout Remediation

Targeted fixes — regrout, recaulk, color-seal, and new movement joints — to extend service life where the structure is still sound. When a repair won’t hold, you’ll hear that too.

Drywall & Wall Prep

Tile and trim only look as good as the walls behind them. Basic repairs and greenboard work keep surfaces ready for the finish you’re paying for.

Prep for a Clean Finish

Repairs, seam taping, finishing, texture matching, and paint-ready surfaces for tile, trim, or cabinet work — especially in baths, kitchens, and utility spaces.
