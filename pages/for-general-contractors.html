How we handle estimates, scheduling, and change orders

Working with busy build teams means clarity from the first email. Here is what you can expect when you bring Tillerstead onto a job:

  • Estimates: Send plans, takeoffs, or site photos. You get a written proposal outlining scope, prep, waterproofing, tile layout notes, and allowances for trims or accessories.
  • Scheduling: Once approved, target dates are confirmed in writing with a readiness checklist covering framing, mechanicals, and materials. You receive confirmations one week and one day before mobilization.
  • Change orders: Scope changes are documented the day they surface with clear pricing and approval signatures so billing matches the work performed.
  • Daily communication: Text or email updates outline progress, upcoming inspections, and what the next trade needs to know before stepping in.
  • Punchlists: Work wraps with photo documentation, cleanup, and a signed completion report to make your closeout painless.
